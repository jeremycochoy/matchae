— The idea

We wanted a spread that
tasted like the matcha we drink.

Most green-coloured spreads on the shelf are white chocolate, palm oil, and a pinch of culinary matcha for the colour. We make ours the opposite way around: ceremonial-grade matcha first, then just enough cocoa butter and raw honey to carry it. No palm oil. No refined sugar. No additives. We called it Aoba — the Japanese word for the green leaf, because that's what each jar is built around.

— Four ingredients

That's the whole list.

01

Ceremonial Uji matcha

First-harvest, stone-ground, from a single Uji farm. The colour and the taste come from here — not from food dye.

02

Organic cocoa butter

Unrefined cocoa butter gives the spread its melt and its silk. No palm oil, ever. Cocoa butter costs more — we think you can taste why.

03

Raw wildflower honey

Single-origin, raw, unfiltered. Honey is the only sweetener. No refined sugar, no syrups, no sweeteners that end in "-ose."

04

A pinch of sea salt

Just enough to round the honey and let the matcha sing. That's it. Four ingredients. Nothing else.
