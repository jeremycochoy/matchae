# Aoba — Marketing Strategy

## Product

**Aoba** — an organic matcha spread that tastes like white chocolate dipped in ceremonial-grade matcha. Cocoa butter base, raw honey sweetening, ceremonial Uji matcha, **zero palm oil**, **zero refined sugar**, **zero bitterness**. Spreadable, soft, naturally green.

Positioning line: *White chocolate, born from the leaf.*

Future SKUs (not promoted yet, mentioned only to set internal direction): hazelnut praliné, dark cocoa hojicha, pistachio shiro.

---

## Five demographic theories (to test on Instagram)

Each theory = a distinct audience + a distinct messaging angle + a distinct visual code. Every post is tagged with the theory it's testing so we can measure engagement per theory and decide which one(s) to amplify with paid ads in phase 2.

### Theory A — "Clean-label millennial" (T-A)

- **Who**: 28–40, urban, educated, dual-income, reads labels at the grocery store, follows accounts like @theofficialminimalbaker, @rainbowplantlife. Buys oat milk, almond butter, no-palm-oil products.
- **Pain**: loves sweet things, hates the trade-off (palm oil, refined sugar, additives).
- **Angle**: ingredient transparency. "Four ingredients. That's it." Side-by-side jar shots vs supermarket spread ingredient lists.
- **Visual code**: clean macro of the jar with all ingredients laid flat next to it (matcha powder, honeycomb, cocoa butter block, jar). White / off-white backgrounds. Sans-serif Helvetica/Inter type.
- **Hashtags**: #cleaneating #noaddedsugar #palmoilfree #realingredients #cleanlabel
- **CTA**: "See every ingredient → link in bio."

### Theory B — "Matcha aesthete" (T-B)

- **Who**: 22–35, specialty-tea curious, follows @ippodotea, @breakawaymatcha, @adagio. Knows the difference between ceremonial and culinary. Owns a chasen.
- **Pain**: matcha sweets are usually too sweet, too bitter, or made with bad matcha. Most "matcha-flavored" things are dyed white chocolate.
- **Angle**: matcha quality, sourcing, terroir. Uji, first harvest, stone-ground, ceremonial grade. Show the matcha, not the sweetness.
- **Visual code**: bamboo whisk, mortar, dark wood, single shaft of light. Vertical compositions. Japanese-influenced typography (sparse, generous whitespace).
- **Hashtags**: #matcha #ceremonialmatcha #ujimatcha #matchalove #tealover
- **CTA**: "Made from Uji ceremonial-grade — link in bio."

### Theory C — "Parents looking for the good treat" (T-C)

- **Who**: 32–48, parents of 4-12 yo, follows @kidseatincolor, @feeding.tiny.foodies. Wants kids to enjoy sweet things without the guilt of Nutella, sugar crashes, food-dye green.
- **Pain**: every kid-friendly spread is either chocolate-hazelnut sugar bomb or sells health on a chalky texture.
- **Angle**: a green spread kids actually beg for. Toast, pancake, dipping fruit. No palm oil. Honey, not sugar.
- **Visual code**: bright, daylit, kitchen counters, children's hands, half-bitten toast. Warm tones. Friendly bubbly script.
- **Hashtags**: #kidsfood #healthysnacks #momlife #healthyforkids #nopalmoil
- **CTA**: "The green spread your kids will ask for — link in bio."

### Theory D — "Wellness / fitness creator" (T-D)

- **Who**: 24–38, follows @drmarkhyman, @maxlugavere, listens to Huberman Lab. Tracks macros loosely. Avoids seed oils, palm oil. Eats Greek yogurt, sourdough, real food.
- **Pain**: wants a sweet topping for yogurt/oats/sourdough that isn't a protein-bar nutritional fiction.
- **Angle**: macros + sourcing. Per-jar callouts: honey-sweetened, cocoa butter, no seed oils, no palm oil. "Eat the ingredients, not a label promise."
- **Visual code**: bright morning light, Greek yogurt, sourdough toast, oat porridge, athletic context. Sharp clean type. Macro breakdown overlays sparingly.
- **Hashtags**: #realfood #seedoilfree #honeysweetened #wellness #fuelyourbody
- **CTA**: "Real food, real fuel — link in bio."

### Theory E — "Foodie / recipe creator" (T-E)

- **Who**: 25–45, posts recipes, follows @halfbakedharvest, @half_baked_harvest, @bromabakery. Cooks for content. Loves novel pairings.
- **Pain**: white chocolate is boring; matcha desserts photograph beautifully but taste mid; needs new pantry items that make content.
- **Angle**: recipe inspiration. Croissant filling, swirl in cheesecake, blondie, matcha-honey toast, cookie sandwich.
- **Visual code**: overhead recipe shots, dough scenes, dripping spoon shots, slow-motion swirls (Reels). Warm restaurant lighting.
- **Hashtags**: #foodphotography #f52grams #matcharecipe #bakingfromscratch #dessertstagram
- **CTA**: "Get the recipe in the caption — full collection on the link in bio."

## Measurement

For each post we will record (in `data/posts.csv`):
- date, time, theory tag, format (image/carousel/reel), caption hook, impressions, reach, profile-visits, link-clicks, follows-attributed, email-signups-attributed.

After 14 days the two theories with the highest **email-signup-per-reach** ratio (not just engagement; engagement is vanity, sign-ups are real intent) become the phase-2 paid-ad creatives.

## Brand voice

- Calm, confident, ingredient-led.
- Never "guilt-free" — we don't apologise for being sweet.
- Never explain the absence of palm oil as a feature you have to ask for; treat it as obvious.
- Always name the matcha source (Uji), the sweetener (raw honey), the fat (cocoa butter).
- Don't compare to Nutella by name; let the visual do it.

## Brand visual

- Logo wordmark: "Aoba" in a thin modern serif, the é accent in matcha-green.
- Primary green: `#5A7A4A` (muted, like aged matcha leaf).
- Cream / off-white: `#F4EFE6`.
- Cocoa accent: `#3D2B1F`.
- Honey gold accent: `#C9A23B`.
- Type pairing: a thin serif display (e.g. Cormorant Garamond) + a clean sans body (Inter).
